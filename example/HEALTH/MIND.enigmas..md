- **Bored? Lacking ideas?** – Keep walking until it becomes fascinating.>- **Hotel Bathroom Principle** – Dress well enough to enter any hotel bathroom without questions; this invites serendipitous encounters.
- **Social Proof** – People won't use a product until they see others using it.
- **Stuck?** – Draw it out.
- **Perspective** – People's opinions matter less; we’re all extra in someone else’s story.
- **Idea Evaluation** – Explore the second and third order consequences; the first idea might not be best.
- **Act Fast** – Procrastination kills flow.
- **Groupthink** – Individuals may sacrifice personal beliefs for consensus.
- **Ad Hominem** – Focus on the idea, not the person.
- **Holistic Thinking** – Avoid choosing outcomes that only reinforce your existing beliefs.
- **Occam’s Razor** – Keep it simple, but don’t oversimplify to the point of losing the essence.
- **Mindset** – Recognize that our understanding isn’t complete; there’s always something missing.
- **Hanlon’s Razor** – Sometimes what appears as malice is just incompetence or limited information.
- **Parkinson’s Law** – Tasks will take as long as you allow them to.
- **Circle of Competence** – Know the limits of what you truly understand.
- **Survivorship Bias** – Look at failures as well as successes.
- **Lindy Effect** – The longer something exists, the more likely it will endure.
- **Repetition** – The more you hear something, the more ingrained it becomes in your beliefs.
- **Dunning-Kruger Effect** – The more you learn, the more you realize how little you know.
- **Pygmalion Effect** – Your performance often mirrors how others perceive you
